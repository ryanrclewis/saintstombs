Saint Vigor was buried, not in the Bayeux cathedral, but in the church community he founded at Mont Chrismat. In 981, Avicien Bayeux fleeing the arrival of the Normans took the bones of St Vigor to the abbey of Saint-Riquier in Picardy. Later, the abbey divided the relics. One arm is at the abbey of Cerisy, a shoulder blade in St Vigor priory, a side Conlie, a tibia Bayeux1, other relics were given by Louis VI at Saint-Vigor of Marly-le-Roi.
